Documentation


GSAn is a new alternative to gene set analysis exploiting the semantic of concepts in the Gene Ontology (GO). Combining data mining and combinatorial algorithms, GSAn provides a reduced and synthetic number of GO terms resuming the biological role of the gene set (or gene product set) input. In the 1st version, GSAn includes the three ontologies of GO: biological process (BP), molecular function (MF) and cellular component (CC). The sections of interest are:

4. Formulas

Information content

The information content or IC is a score associated to the terms explaining how the terms are informative. Bigger is the IC, the term is more specific. Two kind of IC are used in GSAn: intrinsic and extrensic. The IC intrinsic use the information provided from the ontology structure and the IC extrensic using the external support. Seco

Nuno Seco proposed a IC score using the ontology structure. For that, the probability of a term was computed from division between the number of descendants of the term plus the term itself and the number of descendants of root plus 1. The logarithm of this probability provides a no normalized IC by Seco. To normalize the the score between 0 and 1, the IC is divised by the IC maximal that correspond to terms with 0 descendants.
